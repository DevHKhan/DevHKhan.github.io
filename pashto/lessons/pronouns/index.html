Pronouns

Predictably, this lesson will cover pronouns in Pashto. While pronouns often act similarly in both Pashto and English, pronouns in Pashto sometimes work a bit differently than they do in English. We will explore this idea throughout this lesson.

Uninflected and Inflected Pronouns

English Pashto (Uninflected) Pashto (Inflected)
I زه - [zə] ما - [mɑ]
You (singular) ته - [t̪ə] تا - [t̪ɑ]
You (plural) تاسو - [ˈt̪ɑ.so] / تاسې - [ˈt̪ɑ.se] تاسو - [ˈt̪ɑ.so] / تاسې - [ˈt̪ɑ.se]
He/it هغه - [haˈɣa] هغه - [haˈɣə]

We مونږ - [mung] / موږ - [mug] مونږ - [mung] / موږ - [mug]
They هغوی - [haˈɣwi] هغوی - [haˈɣwi]

Inflections? What Are Those?

When we're talking about inflections in languages, we mean changes to word forms that take place due to certain grammatical rules. In general, there are a few rules that tell us when to inflect words in Pashto:

  • If a word is in the company of an adpositional phrase, it will be inflected.
  • If a word serves as the subject of a transitive verb in the past tense, it will be inflected.
  • If a word is plural, it can also receive an inflection.
