Forming Sentences

Now, we will look at how to form full sentences in Pashto.

How Do Sentences Form?

In addition, it is important to remember that Pashto is written from right to left. (The only exception stems from numbers, as they are written from left to right.)

Sentence Structure

Sentences in Pashto follow the SOV (subject-object-verb) structure. This is different from the familiar SVO (subject-verb-object) structure that English uses.

Examples

Below is the color code that I used.

  • Subject
  • Object
  • Verb
  • زه اوبه څښم. - I drink water.
  • ته ۲۶ کتابونه لرې. - You have 26 books.

Wait, This Doesn't Look Familiar!

Don't worry! These examples were only meant to introduce you to the basic sentence structure of Pashto. You will learn more of these vocabulary words later on. For now, just recognize the SVO structure and how the words work together in the above sentences to make that structure work.

Interrogative Sentences

In Pashto, interrogative sentences generally follow the same word order as other sentences in Pashto: SOV. Here are some common question words in Pashto:

  • څنګه؟ - How?
  • څوک؟ - Who?
  • څه؟ - What?
  • ولې؟ - Why?
  • چېرته؟ - Where?
  • کله؟ - When?

Generally, the following rules apply when forming interrogative sentences in Pashto:

  • If there is a subject, put it first before other words. The question word will follow the subject.
  • If the subject is the question word itself, it should still be first in the sentence.
  • If the subject is omitted, the question word will be first.

Examples (Subject First, Question Word After)

  • تا ولې کتاب ولیدلو؟ - Why did you see the book?
